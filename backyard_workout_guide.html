Tempo notation guide
3-1-1
Standard strength
3s lower 1s pause 1s lift
Used in push-ups, rows, squats. The slow eccentric builds tendon strength — critical at 40+.
2-1-2
Controlled bilateral
2s lower 1s pause 2s lift
Used in rows and pulls. Equal time each direction — improves mind-muscle connection.
2-2-1
Paused contraction
2s lower 2s hold 1s lift
Used in hip thrust. The pause at peak removes momentum and forces maximum glute activation.
2-0-1
Continuous tension
2s lower no pause 1s lift
Used in band exercises. No pause keeps band under constant load — maximises time under tension.
3-0-1
Stretch emphasis
3s lower no pause 1s lift
Used in flies and isolation moves. Maximises the stretched position for hypertrophy.
X-X-X
Explosive / ballistic
fast hinge float absorb
KB swings and cleans. Maximum hip-drive power — tempo here is about intent, not counting.
How to read tempo: Three numbers separated by dashes — Eccentric – Pause – Concentric. Eccentric = lowering phase (always controlled). Pause = bottom position hold. Concentric = lifting phase. A 0 means no pause; an X means as fast as possible. The slow eccentric (3s) is the most important number for beginners — connective tissue adapts slower than muscle and needs that time under load.
Desk-job priority note — Pull exercises should outnumber push 2:1. Lead every session with band pull-aparts and face pulls before pressing. The KB swing and hip thrust are the highest-value moves for desk-job glute weakness.
